## 📌 Project Overview

Conducted a comprehensive mutual fund portfolio review 
for a real 3-member family client at Precision Investments. 
The combined family corpus of ₹2,39,38,062 was spread 
across 28 mutual fund schemes with a total investment 
of ₹1,28,67,959.

> **This was not a simulated exercise — this was a real 
> client portfolio with real money and real goals.**

## 🔍 My Analysis Process

### Step 1 — Data Organisation
- Recorded all 28 fund holdings across 3 members
- Added Fund Category, Fund Rating, 3-Year Returns, 
  Risk Level, and Expense Ratio from Value Research Online

### Step 2 — Category-wise Allocation
- Grouped funds by asset class for each member
- Calculated % allocation per category
- Built separate analysis sheets for Father, Mother, Son

### Step 3 — Ideal vs Actual Comparison
- Derived ideal allocation based on each member's 
  goal and risk profile
- Identified gaps between current and recommended 
  allocation
- Flagged overweight and underweight categories

### Step 4 — Goal Feasibility Check
- SWP Corpus Calculation:
  ₹1,20,000 × 12 ÷ 6% = ₹2,40,00,000 required
- Current shortfall: ₹33,72,728 (14% gap)
- Son's required CAGR: 11% over 4 years / 9% over 5 years

### Step 5 — Recommendations
- Built 5 specific actionable recommendations
- Proposed SWP implementation plan

---

## 🚨 Key Findings

### Finding 1 — Father: Dangerous Small Cap Exposure
Father had **37% in Small Cap** against a recommended 
10% for retirement-stage investors. Small caps can 
correct 40-50% in bear markets, directly threatening 
his retirement income plan.

### Finding 2 — Mother: Zero Debt Allocation ⚠️
Mother's entire ₹55,18,639 corpus was in **100% equity** 
with zero debt allocation. Most critical risk finding 
in the entire portfolio. A 30% correction would 
wipe ₹16.5L with no stable buffer.

### Finding 3 — Son: Single Fund Concentration
**66% of Son's corpus in ONE fund** — HDFC Smallcap. 
Zero mid cap allocation. Strong past returns 
(49.8%) masking significant concentration risk.

### Finding 4 — Fund Overlap (Father)
Father held 4 Flexicap funds simultaneously — 
ABSL Flexicap, SBI Flexicap, HDFC Flexicap, 
Parag Parikh Flexicap — all holding largely 
similar large cap stocks.

### Finding 5 — SWP Readiness Gap
Combined parent corpus of ₹2,06,27,272 is 
₹33,72,728 short of the ₹2,40,00,000 needed 
to sustain ₹1.2L/month withdrawal.

## 📝 Key Learnings

1. Strong absolute returns do not mean a 
   well-structured portfolio
2. Asset allocation alignment with goals matters 
   more than individual fund performance
3. Retirement portfolios need stability first, 
   growth second
4. Fund overlap creates false sense of 
   diversification without real benefit
5. SWP sustainability requires corpus planning 
   well before retirement
